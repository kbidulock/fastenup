Child Restraint Safety Tips

  1. Read your manuals!
    • Read your child restraint manual cover to cover. Don’t forget to also read the seat belt, car seat and airbag sections of your vehicle manual. Both manuals contain important safety instructions and information about installing and using your restraint correctly.
  2. Install your restraint using either the seatbelt or UAS (universal anchorage system), not both.
  3. Never install a rear-facing seat in front of an active airbag.
  4. Make sure your restraint is securely installed – it should move no more than 2.5 cm (1 inch) at the belt path when tugged side to side and front to back.
  5. Tether every forward facing car seat to an approved anchor point.
  6. When rear-facing, your child’s harness straps should be located at or below their shoulders. When forward facing, your child’s harness straps should be located at or above their shoulders.
  7. The chest clip should be placed level with your child’s armpits.
  8. Don’t overdress your child - remove bulky layers before buckling to avoid full or partial ejection in a collision.
  9. Conduct the “pinch” test at your child’s collarbone to make sure the harness is snug. If you can pinch a horizontal fold in the harness webbing, the harness is too loose.
  10. Never use an expired, recalled or damaged seat.
  11. Be wary of purchasing used seats - it is important to know your car seat's history as your child’s life is in the hands of the previous owner.
  12. Make sure your child is riding in the right restraint for their age, height and weight. Child restraints are not milestones – don’t rush your child into their next stage too soon:
    • Rear face as long as possible (at least age 2).
    • Consider proper belt fit and maturity before moving to a booster seat (minimum ages 5 and up).
    • Children must pass the 5 step test before they can safely ride without a booster (usually between ages 10-12).
    • Kids are safest in the back. Keep all children riding in the back seat until they are 13 years old.

What to expect at an appointment:

My goal is to educate and empower you so that you will leave the appointment confident in your abilities to install and use your child restraint correctly!

Please bring both your car seat and vehicle manuals along to your appointment – we will reference them during the process.

Know your child’s height and weight, and if possible, bring your child along to your appointment.

Expect your appointment to be an interactive one and be prepared to learn. CPSAC technicians are trained to teach you so that you will be comfortable reinstalling and adjusting your restraint as necessary.
